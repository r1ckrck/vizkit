# Test Brief — The Design Engineer's Stack

A multi-format generation brief for vizkit testing. One overarching topic rendered across image, video, GIF, and infographic, all visually and conceptually related.

---

## Topic

A working stack of tools that defines how a design engineer makes things. Reads as a tower of layers — each layer a named slab.

---

## The stack (consistent across formats)

From foundation upward:

1. **Production** — where shipped work lives
2. **Markdowns** — text-based knowledge, design system, documentation, all stored in plain markdown
3. **Skills** — named modular capabilities. Notable ones: figma-use, markset, docx, pptx, fal-video, frontend-design, code-reviewer, code-simplifier
4. **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** — the connective tissue. The "hands" layer. External tools like Paper and Figma plug into the workflow through here.
5. **Paper** — AI-native design canvas. Real HTML/CSS, not SVG. Bidirectional with code through MCP.
6. **fal.ai** — generative media (images, video, GIFs)
7. **Figma** — design source-of-truth. Connected via MCP.
8. **Claude Code IDE** — the workspace where it all happens

---

## Image — vertical stack poster

A poster of the stack as a tower of horizontal slabs, one per layer, eight layers tall.

Each slab is labeled, characterized, and visually distinct — different heights to reflect weight, different micro-textures or treatments — but all unified by a single typographic and color system. A spine runs up the side connecting the slabs. Hairline annotations in the margin call out one short word or fragment per layer.

The MCP slab visually reads as connective — its annotations on the side hint at threads running out to the Paper and Figma slabs that sit nearby. The Skills slab carries small badges with named examples.

Mood: editorial design poster. Architectural. The kind of piece you'd see on a studio wall or in a design exhibition, not a developer marketing page. Negative space is part of the composition, not absence of it.

Hierarchy reads in a glance: tower of layers. Closer reading rewards with the annotations, slab textures, and the named skill badges.

---

## Video — 5 seconds, animated stack assembly

The same stack assembling layer by layer, from foundation up. Each layer arrives with a small grounded entrance — settle, snap, or fade — and its label appears as the slab lands.

When MCP arrives, two short threads animate out from it to where Paper and Figma will land in the next beats — implying the connection before those layers are even present.

Pacing: roughly half a second per layer, with a brief hold at the end. The viewer feels the stack settling into place, never rushing.

Mood: weighted, considered motion. No spins, no zoom-ins, no transitional flourish. Layers behave like physical blocks finding their place.

Final frame matches the poster image, held for the closing beat.

---

## GIF — same concept, fresh generation, looping

The same stack assembly, looping cleanly. The poster end-state is the rest frame; the loop returns by the layers either fading or breaking down before re-assembling — never a hard jump-cut back to the start.

Mood matches the video: quiet, weighted, no GIF-y flourish.

---

## Infographic — dense, intricate, compressed

Where the poster is a single tower, this is a city. A multi-section information map of the same stack, but each layer expanded with sub-elements, surrounded by supporting diagrams and annotations, read as one composition.

Sections to include, each its own visual unit clearly delineated:

- The eight stack layers from the poster, each one expanded:
  - **Skills** broken into its named examples — figma-use, markset, docx, pptx, fal-video, frontend-design, code-reviewer, code-simplifier — each with a one-line role descriptor
  - **Markdowns** broken into types of docs they hold (system rules, project notes, design tokens, voice & design language, captures)
  - **MCP** broken into the active connections — Paper, Figma, and others — with a small "what flows through this connection" annotation per arrow
  - **Paper** with a short note on its bidirectional canvas and HTML/CSS native foundation
- A workflow timeline showing how a single piece of work moves through the stack
- A side panel labeling **input → process → output** at each layer
- A small connector diagram showing how Claude Code orchestrates across layers, with MCP threads running between the relevant slabs
- A mini-legend defining the symbols used elsewhere on the piece
- Margin annotations — ratios, percentages, frequencies where they make sense
- Small trade-off boxes attached to each layer
- A footer strip with influences, references, and a version date

Mood: 1970s technical reference poster, modernized. A fold-out from a design manual, or an architectural cross-section. Every inch carries information; every section earns its real estate. White space is composed, not abundant.

Type treatment: multiple weights and sizes, clear hierarchy. Numbered or lettered sub-sections. Hairlines for connectors. Icons only where they do semantic work.

Density target: a viewer scans the whole thing and grasps the system shape in 3 seconds, then spends 2 minutes finding details. That's the reward loop.
